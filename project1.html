The project focused on implementing and optimizing a Quality Testing algorithm for Contact Lenses. The algorithm aimed to enhance the quality assurance process in contact lens production by accurately determining the rotation angle of the lens from a pair of high-definition images. The primary data source for the project was a set of high-definition images representing individual contact lenses before and after undergoing a random rotation. The rotation was induced by a machine, causing the lens to rotate at an arbitrary angle. The objective was to develop an algorithm that could accurately determine the rotation angle, which is crucial for identifying defects and impurities on the lens. Initially, the ORB (Oriented FAST and Rotated BRIEF) algorithm was used to find keypoints in the contact lens image and match them to calculate the rotation angle. However, due to the challenge of distinguishing lens defects from impurities in the water, a corresponding NCC (Normalized Cross Correlation) algorithm was implemented. This algorithm leveraged the imperfect shape of the contact lens and compared the imperfect contour in pre- and post-rotation images to calculate the rotation angle. The implemented algorithm achieved an accuracy rate of 69.8% in accurately determining the rotation angle for many lens pairs. However, there is still room for improvement, and future work will focus on enhancing the accuracy and robustness of the algorithm.
